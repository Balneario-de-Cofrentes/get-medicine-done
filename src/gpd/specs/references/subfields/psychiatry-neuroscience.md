# Psychiatry & Neuroscience

## Core Concepts and Terminology

**Diagnostic Systems:**

- **DSM-5-TR (2022):** Diagnostic and Statistical Manual; categorical diagnoses; used primarily in US; published by APA
- **ICD-11 (2022):** International Classification of Diseases, 11th revision; WHO; required for international reporting
- **RDoC (Research Domain Criteria):** NIMH dimensional framework; cross-cutting constructs (negative valence, positive valence, cognitive, social, arousal/modulatory, sensorimotor); not diagnostic — research framework
- **HiTOP (Hierarchical Taxonomy of Psychopathology):** Empirically derived dimensional model of psychopathology; spectra (internalizing, externalizing, thought disorder, detachment, somatoform, antagonistic)

**Major Diagnostic Categories:**

- **Mood disorders:** Major depressive disorder (MDD), persistent depressive disorder, bipolar I (mania + depression), bipolar II (hypomania + depression)
- **Anxiety disorders:** Generalized anxiety disorder (GAD), panic disorder, social anxiety disorder, specific phobias, agoraphobia
- **Psychotic disorders:** Schizophrenia, schizoaffective disorder, delusional disorder, brief psychotic disorder
- **Trauma/stressor-related:** PTSD, acute stress disorder, adjustment disorders
- **Neurodevelopmental:** ADHD, autism spectrum disorder, intellectual disability, specific learning disorders
- **Substance use disorders:** Severity by DSM-5 criteria count (2-3 mild, 4-5 moderate, 6+ severe)
- **Neurocognitive disorders:** Delirium, major and mild neurocognitive disorders (Alzheimer's, vascular, Lewy body, frontotemporal)

**Outcome Measures:**

- **Depression:** PHQ-9 (screening/severity; >=10 moderate), HAM-D (clinician-rated; 17 or 21 items), MADRS (clinician-rated; 10 items), BDI-II (self-report)
- **Anxiety:** GAD-7 (screening; >=10 moderate), HAM-A (clinician-rated), STAI (state-trait)
- **Psychosis:** PANSS (Positive and Negative Syndrome Scale; 30 items), BPRS (Brief Psychiatric Rating Scale), CGI (Clinical Global Impression)
- **Functioning:** GAF (Global Assessment of Functioning; now removed from DSM-5), WHODAS 2.0 (WHO Disability Assessment Schedule)
- **Quality of life:** WHOQOL-BREF, SF-36, EQ-5D
- **Response criteria:** Typically >=50% reduction in symptom scale; remission = below threshold (e.g., HAM-D <=7, PHQ-9 <5)

**Psychopharmacology:**

- **Antidepressants:** SSRIs (first-line), SNRIs, TCAs, MAOIs, atypicals (mirtazapine, bupropion, vortioxetine, esketamine); onset typically 2-4 weeks; full effect 6-8 weeks
- **Antipsychotics:** First-generation/typical (haloperidol, chlorpromazine) — D2 blockade, EPS risk; second-generation/atypical (risperidone, olanzapine, quetiapine, aripiprazole, clozapine) — broader receptor profile, metabolic risk
- **Mood stabilizers:** Lithium (gold standard for bipolar; narrow TI, TDM required); valproate, carbamazepine, lamotrigine
- **Anxiolytics:** Benzodiazepines (short-term only; dependence risk), buspirone, pregabalin (some countries)
- **Clozapine:** Only evidence-based treatment for treatment-resistant schizophrenia; requires blood monitoring (agranulocytosis risk ~1%)

**Psychotherapy:**

- **Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT):** First-line for depression, anxiety, PTSD, OCD; evidence base is extensive
- **Interpersonal Therapy (IPT):** Efficacious for depression; focuses on interpersonal problems
- **EMDR:** Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing; evidence-based for PTSD
- **Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT):** For borderline personality disorder; combines CBT with mindfulness
- **Psychodynamic therapy:** Long-term personality change; evidence growing but weaker than CBT for specific disorders
- **Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT):** Third-wave CBT; psychological flexibility

## Key Research Methodologies

**Clinical Trials in Psychiatry:**

- Placebo response is high (30-40% in depression trials); large sample sizes needed
- Active comparator (head-to-head) trials increasingly demanded by regulators and guidelines
- Measurement-based care: systematic symptom measurement at each visit
- Run-in periods: sometimes used to exclude placebo responders (controversial — reduces generalizability)
- Sequential treatment designs: STAR*D model (steps for non-responders)
- Network meta-analysis critical for comparing many treatments (Cipriani et al. 2018 for antidepressants)

**Neuroimaging:**

- **Structural MRI:** Gray matter volume (VBM), cortical thickness (FreeSurfer), white matter tracts (DTI/DWI)
- **Functional MRI (fMRI):** BOLD signal as proxy for neural activity; task-based (activation during cognitive task) or resting-state (functional connectivity)
- **PET:** Receptor occupancy (D2, 5-HT), glucose metabolism (FDG), amyloid/tau (Alzheimer's biomarkers)
- **EEG/MEG:** High temporal resolution; event-related potentials (ERPs); spectral analysis (alpha, beta, gamma, theta)
- **Statistical pitfalls:** Multiple comparisons (voxel-wise FWE or FDR correction), circular analysis ("double dipping"), small samples, reverse inference (inferring mental state from activation)
- **Reproducibility crisis:** Many fMRI findings have not replicated; large samples (N>100 for individual differences) now expected

**Neuropsychological Assessment:**

- Standardized test batteries: WAIS-IV (intelligence), WMS-IV (memory), Trail Making (executive function), Stroop (inhibition), Wisconsin Card Sorting (cognitive flexibility)
- Normative data adjusted for age, education, sex
- Ecological validity: laboratory tests may not predict real-world function
- Computerized batteries: CANTAB, CogState; advantages in standardization and sensitivity

## Common Study Designs

| Design | Application | Key Consideration |
|--------|------------|-------------------|
| Placebo-controlled RCT | Drug efficacy | High placebo response; large N needed |
| Active comparator RCT | Head-to-head drug comparison | Non-inferiority or superiority |
| Psychotherapy RCT | Therapy efficacy | Therapist as random effect; treatment fidelity |
| Platform trial | Multiple treatments for MDD | Shared placebo arm, adaptive randomization |
| Pragmatic trial | Real-world effectiveness | Broad eligibility, routine measures |
| Cohort (longitudinal) | Risk factors, trajectories | Prodromal states, transition to psychosis |
| Cross-sectional neuroimaging | Brain-behavior correlations | Large sample needed; replication essential |
| N-of-1 | Individualized medication | Repeated crossover, patient-centered |

## Verification Considerations

**Diagnostic Validity:**

- Check: is the correct diagnostic system specified (DSM-5, ICD-11)?
- Check: are structured diagnostic interviews used (SCID, MINI, CIDI) or clinical diagnosis only?
- Check: are comorbid diagnoses identified and addressed?

**Treatment Effect Assessment:**

- Check: is the placebo response rate reported? Effect size should be interpreted relative to placebo
- Check: is response distinguished from remission? (>=50% improvement vs below clinical threshold)
- Check: are effect sizes clinically meaningful? (SMD of 0.3 for antidepressants may represent modest clinical benefit)
- Check: NNT reported alongside NNH to assess benefit-risk balance

**Neuroimaging Quality:**

- Check: is sample size adequate? (N<30 per group is generally underpowered for fMRI)
- Check: are multiple comparisons corrected (FWE, FDR, cluster-based)?
- Check: is reverse inference avoided (cannot infer emotion from amygdala activation alone)?
- Check: are confounds controlled (motion, medication, age, sex)?
- Check: is the analysis pre-registered?

**Psychotherapy Research:**

- Check: is treatment fidelity assessed (recorded sessions, adherence checklists)?
- Check: are therapist effects modeled (therapist as random effect)?
- Check: is the control condition described (waitlist, attention control, active comparator)?
- Check: are outcome assessors blinded?

## Common Pitfalls and LLM Error Risks

- **Chemical imbalance narrative:** LLMs may perpetuate the oversimplified "serotonin deficiency causes depression" model, which is not supported by current evidence
- **Overinterpreting neuroimaging:** Brain activation differences between groups do not establish causation, diagnosis, or mechanism. LLMs may present fMRI findings as more definitive than warranted
- **Confusing efficacy data across disorders:** Antidepressant effect sizes and mechanisms differ by disorder. LLMs may generalize from MDD data to anxiety or vice versa
- **Fabricating study results:** LLMs generate plausible but fictitious meta-analysis results, NNTs, and effect sizes for psychiatric treatments. Always verify
- **Ignoring the placebo effect:** Psychiatric placebo response rates are high. LLMs may present response rates without placebo context, inflating apparent drug efficacy
- **Overstating genetic determinism:** Psychiatric disorders are polygenic with small individual variant effects. LLMs may present genetic findings as more predictive than they are
- **Misrepresenting psychotherapy evidence:** LLMs may understate psychotherapy evidence or present it as inferior to medication when meta-analyses show comparable efficacy for many conditions
- **Confusing DSM editions:** LLMs may mix criteria from different DSM versions (DSM-IV vs DSM-5)
- **Ignoring cultural context:** Psychiatric diagnosis and treatment vary significantly across cultures. LLMs typically present a Western psychiatric perspective without qualification
- **Serotonin syndrome misdiagnosis criteria:** LLMs may provide incorrect Hunter or Sternbach criteria for serotonin syndrome

## Methodology Decision Tree

```
What is the psychiatry/neuroscience research question?
├── Drug efficacy
│   ├── New drug? → Placebo-controlled + active comparator RCT
│   ├── Existing drugs comparison? → Head-to-head RCT or NMA
│   └── Treatment-resistant? → Sequential treatment design (STAR*D model)
├── Psychotherapy efficacy
│   ├── Vs waitlist? → RCT with attention control preferred
│   ├── Vs drug? → RCT with both arms + combination
│   └── Optimal format? → Comparative RCT (individual vs group, online vs in-person)
├── Neuroimaging
│   ├── Brain structure/function differences? → Case-control with adequate N (>100)
│   ├── Treatment mechanism? → Pre/post imaging within RCT
│   └── Biomarker development? → Large cohort, replication sample
├── Risk factors / trajectories
│   ├── Prospective? → Longitudinal cohort from at-risk population
│   ├── Genetic? → GWAS consortium (PGC)
│   └── Environmental? → Quasi-experimental (natural experiments, MR)
└── Evidence synthesis
    ├── Treatment comparison? → NMA (Cipriani methodology)
    ├── Neuroimaging? → Coordinate-based meta-analysis (ALE, SDM)
    └── Prognostic? → IPD meta-analysis
```
