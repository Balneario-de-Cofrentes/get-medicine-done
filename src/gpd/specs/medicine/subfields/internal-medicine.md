# Internal Medicine

## Core Concepts and Terminology

**Cardiology:**

- **Heart failure classification:** NYHA functional classes I-IV; HFrEF (EF<40%), HFmrEF (40-49%), HFpEF (>=50%)
- **Guideline-directed medical therapy (GDMT) for HFrEF:** Four pillars — RAAS inhibitor (ACEi/ARB/ARNI), beta-blocker, MRA, SGLT2 inhibitor
- **Acute coronary syndromes:** STEMI (ST elevation), NSTEMI (troponin rise, no ST elevation), unstable angina (no troponin rise); GRACE score for risk stratification
- **Atrial fibrillation:** CHA2DS2-VASc score for stroke risk; HAS-BLED for bleeding risk; DOACs preferred over warfarin for most patients
- **Hypertension:** ACC/AHA threshold 130/80 mmHg; ESC/ESH threshold 140/90 mmHg with treatment targets varying by age and comorbidities
- **Valvular disease:** Aortic stenosis (AVR thresholds based on gradient, velocity, symptom status); mitral regurgitation (primary vs secondary); TAVR vs SAVR decision-making

**Pulmonology:**

- **COPD:** GOLD classification by FEV1 (stages 1-4); ABE assessment by symptoms and exacerbation history; triple therapy (ICS/LABA/LAMA) for high-risk patients
- **Asthma:** GINA stepwise approach; ICS cornerstone of treatment; biologics (anti-IgE, anti-IL5, anti-IL4R) for severe asthma
- **Interstitial lung diseases:** UIP pattern (IPF), NSIP, organizing pneumonia; antifibrotics (pirfenidone, nintedanib) for IPF
- **Pulmonary embolism:** Wells score, Geneva score; D-dimer for exclusion; CTPA for diagnosis; PESI for risk stratification
- **Spirometry interpretation:** FEV1/FVC <0.70 (or LLN) = obstruction; reduced FVC with normal ratio = possible restriction (confirm with TLC); bronchodilator reversibility >12% and >200mL suggests asthma

**Nephrology:**

- **CKD staging:** GFR-based (G1-G5) and albuminuria-based (A1-A3); KDIGO heat map for risk
- **AKI:** KDIGO staging by creatinine rise or urine output; pre-renal, intrinsic, post-renal
- **Glomerular diseases:** Nephrotic syndrome (proteinuria >3.5 g/day), nephritic syndrome (hematuria, hypertension, oliguria); diagnosis often requires renal biopsy
- **Dialysis:** Hemodialysis vs peritoneal dialysis; initiation based on symptoms, not GFR threshold alone; Kt/V for adequacy
- **Renal protective therapies:** RAAS blockade, SGLT2 inhibitors (DAPA-CKD, EMPA-KIDNEY), finerenone; BP control <130/80

**Endocrinology:**

- **Diabetes mellitus:** Type 1 (autoimmune, insulin-dependent), Type 2 (insulin resistance; metformin first-line, then GLP-1 RA or SGLT2i based on comorbidities)
- **HbA1c targets:** Generally <7% (53 mmol/mol); individualized (tighter for young, looser for frail elderly)
- **Thyroid:** TSH screening; hypothyroidism (levothyroxine); hyperthyroidism (Graves: thionamides, RAI, surgery; toxic nodule: RAI or surgery)
- **Adrenal:** Addison's disease (primary insufficiency); Cushing's syndrome (confirm with 24h UFC, late-night salivary cortisol, DST)
- **Osteoporosis:** T-score <= -2.5 by DXA; FRAX for fracture risk; bisphosphonates first-line; denosumab, teriparatide, romosozumab for high-risk

**Gastroenterology:**

- **IBD:** Crohn's disease (transmural, any GI location) vs ulcerative colitis (mucosal, colon only); biologics (anti-TNF, vedolizumab, ustekinumab) for moderate-severe
- **Cirrhosis:** Child-Pugh (A/B/C) and MELD scores; variceal bleeding prophylaxis; HCC screening (ultrasound + AFP every 6 months)
- **GERD:** PPI trial; alarm features (dysphagia, weight loss, GI bleeding) require endoscopy; Barrett's esophagus surveillance
- **Celiac disease:** TTG-IgA screening; duodenal biopsy for confirmation; gluten-free diet is the treatment
- **Hepatitis:** HBV (tenofovir, entecavir for chronic); HCV (DAAs achieve >95% SVR); NAFLD/NASH (lifestyle, pioglitazone, vitamin E; resmetirom for NASH)

**Rheumatology:**

- **Rheumatoid arthritis:** Classification criteria (ACR/EULAR 2010); treat-to-target (DAS28 remission); methotrexate first-line, biologics for refractory
- **SLE:** SLEDAI/BILAG for disease activity; hydroxychloroquine for all patients; belimumab, voclosporin, anifrolumab for active disease
- **Gout:** Serum urate target <6 mg/dL (<5 mg/dL with tophi); acute: colchicine, NSAIDs, steroids; chronic: allopurinol, febuxostat
- **Axial spondyloarthritis:** HLA-B27, sacroiliac MRI; NSAIDs first-line, then TNF inhibitors or IL-17 inhibitors

## Key Research Methodologies

**Diagnostic Accuracy Studies:**

- Sensitivity, specificity, PPV, NPV; ROC analysis with AUC
- STARD reporting guideline for diagnostic studies
- Spectrum bias: test performance varies with disease severity
- Verification bias: not all patients receive the reference standard

**Prognostic Studies:**

- Risk prediction models: development (derivation), validation (internal + external), updating
- TRIPOD reporting guideline for prediction models
- Calibration (predicted vs observed) as important as discrimination (C-statistic)
- Net reclassification improvement (NRI) and decision curve analysis for model comparison

**Practice Guidelines:**

- GRADE-based guidelines (WHO, NICE, AHA/ACC) rate evidence certainty and recommendation strength
- Strong vs conditional recommendations; high vs low certainty evidence
- Living guidelines: continuously updated as new evidence emerges

## Verification Considerations

**Guideline Currency:**

- Check: is the guideline version current? Guidelines update frequently (especially cardiology, diabetes)
- Check: have landmark trials published since the guideline that might change recommendations?
- Check: are different guidelines consistent? (ACC/AHA vs ESC may differ, e.g., BP thresholds)

**Diagnostic Criteria Precision:**

- Check: are the correct diagnostic criteria applied (e.g., ACR/EULAR 2010 for RA, not older criteria)?
- Check: are classification criteria (for research) distinguished from diagnostic criteria (for clinical practice)?

**Drug-Disease Interactions:**

- Check: are contraindications and cautions correctly identified?
- Check: is renal dosing addressed for medications cleared by the kidney?
- Check: are drug interactions (especially CYP450) considered?

**Outcome Validity:**

- Check: are surrogate endpoints (HbA1c, LDL, BP) distinguished from hard endpoints (mortality, MI, stroke)?
- Check: has the surrogate been validated as predicting the hard outcome?

## Common Pitfalls and LLM Error Risks

- **Outdated guidelines:** LLMs may cite older guideline versions, especially for rapidly evolving fields like heart failure and diabetes. Always check the latest edition
- **Conflating classification and diagnostic criteria:** Classification criteria (RA, SLE) are designed for research and have high specificity; clinical diagnosis may not require meeting full classification criteria
- **Wrong drug dosing:** LLMs may provide incorrect medication doses, especially for renal-adjusted dosing. Always verify against formulary
- **Surrogate endpoint over-interpretation:** LLMs may present improvements in surrogate markers as proof of clinical benefit without noting the distinction
- **Fabricating trial names and results:** LLMs generate plausible but fictitious trial names (acronyms), sample sizes, and results. Always verify against PubMed/ClinicalTrials.gov
- **Confusing HFrEF and HFpEF treatment:** Treatment evidence differs dramatically between HFrEF and HFpEF. LLMs may apply HFrEF evidence to HFpEF
- **Missing multimorbidity interactions:** Internal medicine patients rarely have one disease. LLMs may give disease-specific advice without considering comorbid conditions
- **Incorrect normal ranges:** Laboratory reference ranges vary by assay, population, and laboratory. LLMs may provide incorrect cut-offs

## Methodology Decision Tree

```
What is the internal medicine research question?
├── Treatment efficacy
│   ├── New drug? → Phase III RCT with hard endpoints
│   ├── Treatment strategy comparison? → Pragmatic RCT or registry-based RCT
│   └── Rare disease? → Small RCT or N-of-1, consider adaptive design
├── Diagnosis
│   ├── New biomarker? → Diagnostic accuracy study (STARD)
│   └── Clinical prediction rule? → Derivation cohort + validation cohort (TRIPOD)
├── Prognosis
│   ├── Risk model? → Prospective cohort, external validation
│   └── Long-term outcomes? → Registry analysis, survival modeling
├── Evidence summary
│   ├── Single comparison? → Systematic review + meta-analysis (PRISMA)
│   └── Multiple treatments? → Network meta-analysis
└── Quality of care
    ├── Guideline adherence? → Clinical audit, registry analysis
    └── Intervention to improve care? → Cluster RCT or stepped-wedge
```
